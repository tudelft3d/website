Welcome to the 3D Geoinformation Group!

We're excited to have you join us!

So, you recently joined our group! The first days can be a little overwhelming, so we put together a TODO list to ease the process:

ACCESS

Get a NetID and CampusCard (our secretaries Margo or Martine will arrange this for you). You will need your campus card to access our office and – more importantly – to get coffee!

COMMUNICATION

Download Telegram (if you don't already have it) and ask anyone in the group to add you to our group chat. It's also helpful if you have a global username so that it's easier to add you to group chats. This is our main communication channel as a group (even though other people in TU Delft are using Teams). You can find your colleagues through the member list of our main channel (3DGeoInfo). Please check your messages regularly!

DESK

Do you know where your desk is? If not, check here to see if a desk has been assigned to you. If not, ask Amy or Gina.

GITHUB

Gina or your supervisor can give you access to the repositories of our organization. Be sure to accept the invitation when you receive it. You will need access to the GitHub organization for various steps below.

WEBSITE

When you have time, you should update our website with your info on the 'staff' page.

You will need to clone the website repository, follow the instructions and update the _data/staff.yml file (or the _data/staff_visiting.yml file if you are visiting). The website gets updated automatically (after a few minutes) when you push your changes to the 'main' branch.

You will need the following information:

  • Full name
  • Email
  • Position/title
  • Institute/University (if you are visiting)
  • A square photo of 200X200px (This is important because we need to keep the size of the repo small)
  • A one-line description of your main project
  • A lengthier description of your research and background for your webpage (if you already have a webpage and you need to host it, it can also be done)
  • Link to website/LinkedIn

It would also be nice if you could write a short welcoming post for yourself (not necessary for academic visitors). Please have a look at previous posts for some inspiration.

If you don't have time or encounter any difficulties, Gina can help you!

MAILING LIST

Subscribe to our group's mailing list. With it, you'll receive all important emails and invites. You can also email everyone in the group at 3dgeoinfo-bk@lists.tudelft.nl. Please make sure that your supervisor sends you invitations to any upcoming but earlier-organised group events and meetings.

WORKING REMOTELY

Set up your TU Delft VPN for working off campus. The instruction manual for the configuration can be found here.

3DTEA

In the 3D geoinformation group we have a casual weekly meeting which we call 3DTea. This happens every Thursday at 16:00 and people can attend either online or in person. This time is usually used for short presentations about recent work/research, or about other matters that might interest us. As a newcomer, you will use one of the 3DTeas to introduce yourself and talk about the work you will be doing in the group. Most importantly, you need to include information about your favourite dish, favourite music, and favourite book/movie/series etc. Please see the available dates here or ask Amy.

UDS MONTHLY CATCH UP

Once per month we meet with the whole Urban Data Science section to update each other about our work. For this meeting we store the minutes in [this repo](https://github.com/tudelft3d/UDS-catch-up). Prior to the meeting you will get a notification on Telegram to add your updates. Please make sure to fill it in before the meeting!

WEEKLY UPDATES

Every Monday you will receive a reminder on the 3DGeoInfo channel on Telegram (from our bot, Wynn) to update the rest of the group on what you are working on this week. You should go to 'issues' in this repo, and you will see a "Weekly updates" issue where you can add your updates.

HOLIDAYS

If you need to request holidays, you can do this through My TIM. You should add your holidays (or other periods you are away) to our shared group calendar. Ask your supervisor or Gina to give you access to it. All staff members (specifically PhDs, Postdocs, and other project researchers) are requested to take 5 weeks of holidays per year (for example, 1 week in spring, 3 weeks during summer, and 1 week in autumn). You can discuss this in more detail with your supervisors, but you are always encouraged to take most of your vacation days.

SERVER ACCESS

Get access to our servers, if you need it (GitHub Access required).

STORAGE

Get informed about your storage options and the computation options (GitHub Access required).

LAPTOPS AND EQUIPMENT

Please arrange to get a new laptop through your supervisor and the secretaries. If you need other equipment like a screen or a keyboard, Gina can help you place an order. If you have already received your laptop or other equipment with a TU Delft tag, please make sure to update this inventory (GitHub Access required).

CODE AND DATA GUIDELINES

Make sure you are familiar with our guidelines for your code and data management (GitHub Access required).
