Urban Energy Laboratory

Welcome!

 The Urbanism Energy Laboratory is embedded within the 3D Geoinformation group, which is part of the Department of Urbanism at TU Delft's Faculty of Architecture and Built Environment.

We are an international group of researchers working on heterogenous topics mainly dealing with energy in the context of the built environment. In particular, we focus on tacking challenges that relate to urban suistainability, urban transition and the on-going digitalisation process.

Together with our TU-Delft and international colleagues we also investigate how and in which cases Urban Digital Twins can help and support different stakeholders (e.g. citizens, municipalities, planners) in the co-creation and co-development process to jointly design and implement urban solutions, therefore moving from top-down planning to bottom-up approaches.




Research topics and vision

 Our research interests span over the fields of urban digital twinning (based on semantic 3D city models), urban-scale energy simulation, data modelling and standardisation. The leitmotif is to foster software and data integration to allow complex urban system simulations and to bridge the the gap between geo-information and urban planning in order to support engineers, architects, urban planners - and ultimately citizens! - in their daily activities, eventually contributing to reshaping our homes and cities to be better liveable and sustainable.

We strongly believe in open-science by pushing open-access publications, developing open-source software, reproducible methodologies and results in order to maximise our impact on society.
